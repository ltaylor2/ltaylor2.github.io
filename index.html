I'm a first-year Ph.D. student (NSF GRFP) in the Ecology and Evolutionary Biology Department at Yale University. My current research interests include sexual selection theory (how does "Beauty Happen"?) and applying computational theory and tools to the study of birds in the lab and in the field.

Some past research projects include:

  • Behavior of Golden-winged Manakins in the cloudforests of Ecuador
  • Demographics of Semipalmated Plovers in the subartic of Churchill, Manitoba
  • Demographics of Tree Swallows on Kent Island
  • Two seasons learning the trade with Leach's Storm-petrels on Kent Island

Some other research includes CS work in computer vision, spatial reasoning, and machine learning.

I'm also a passable birder, a miserable taker-of-bird-photos, and a new knitter.
