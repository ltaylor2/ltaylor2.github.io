I'm a first-year Ph.D. student (NSF GRFP) in the Ecology and Evolutionary Biology Department at Yale University. My graduate research will focus on merging sexual selection theory with phylogenetic modeling and the behavioral ecology of mating displays in manakins (Pipridae). I am excited to link my interests in sexual selection research with broader social reproductive systems in birds (such as the coordination of biparental care). With a background in computer science, I also pursue research opportunities in complex modeling approaches (particularly for avian demography) and computational tools for fieldwork (such as machine vision and machine learning).

Some past fieldwork includes:

  • Golden-winged Manakins in the cloud forests of Ecuador
  • Semipalmated Plovers in the subartic of Churchill, Manitoba
  • Two seasons learning the trade with Leach's Storm-petrels on Kent Island, New Brunswick, Canada

I'm also an OK birder, a miserable taker-of-bird-photos, and a collector of rustic-seeming hobbies.
