I'm a third-year Ph.D. candidate (NSF GRFP) in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at Yale University. My graduate research (with Dr. Richard Prum) focuses on avian adolesence, deferred reproduction, and the evolution of delayed plumage maturation in birds. I also have an interest in the intersection of biology and cultural anthropology; I'm curious about what biologists mean (and what they think they mean) when they say that something means something. With a background in computer science, I try to pursue complex modeling approaches whenever I can remember how to change the working directory in Python.

Some current research projects include:

  • An effort to situate our understanding of delayed plumage maturation within the broader frameoworks of life history theory, sexual selection theory, and evo-devo.
  • An attempt to frame the role of social agency (genuine telos!) in Sexual and Artifical Selection.
  • A long-running project using object-oriented models to determine energetic expectations for biparental incubation behavior in Leach's Storm-petrels (Oceanodroma leucorhoa)
  • Some work on population structure and phylogenetics using ddRAD-seq data. Right now, I'm working to investigate species boundaries in a tropical thrush with a highly disjunct distribution.

    • Some past fieldwork includes:

    • Golden-winged Manakins in the cloud forests of Ecuador
    • Semipalmated Plovers in the subartic of Churchill, Manitoba
    • Two seasons with Leach's Storm-petrels on Kent Island, New Brunswick

    I'm also an OK birder (think companion-species, not rare-bird-alert) and a collector of rustic-seeming hobbies.
